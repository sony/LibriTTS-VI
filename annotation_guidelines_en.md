# Voice Impression Annotation Guidelines

This document defines the criteria for evaluating the perceptual impression of voices. All evaluations are conducted on a 7-point scale.

---

### 1. Pitch (Low - High)

-   **Scale:** Low (1) - High (7)
-   **Overview:** This is an evaluation of the acoustic pitch of the voice. Please rate the initial impression of the voice's pitch as if you were being greeted for the first time. Try to disregard the speaker's gender.
-   **Reference Audio (Neutral):** `792_127528_000040_000002.wav`
-   **Evaluation Criteria:**
    -   **1 (Low):** A very low-pitched voice. A sound that resonates in the chest and is perceived as having a low frequency.
    -   **4 (Neutral):** A voice of average pitch, neither high nor low. Generally, male voices tend to be lower and female voices higher.
    -   **7 (High):** A very high-pitched voice, like that of a soprano singer. A sound perceived as having a high frequency, such as a child's voice.

---

### 2. Gender (Masculine - Feminine)

-   **Scale:** Masculine (1) - Feminine (7)
-   **Overview:** This is an evaluation of the gender impression conveyed by the voice. Please rate based solely on the impression from the voice, not the speaker's actual gender. Imagine the gender you would picture if you received a phone call from this person.
-   **Reference Audio (Neutral):** `792_127528_000040_000002.wav`
-   **Evaluation Criteria:**
    -   **1 (Masculine):** A voice with strong characteristics typically perceived as "masculine" (e.g., thickness, low pitch).
    -   **4 (Neutral):** A voice with a neutral impression, difficult to classify as either masculine or feminine.
    -   **7 (Feminine):** A voice with strong characteristics typically perceived as "feminine" (e.g., brightness, high pitch).

---

### 3. Clarity (Clear - Hoarse)

-   **Scale:** Clear (1) - Hoarse (7)
-   **Overview:** This is an evaluation of the voice's clarity and lack of noise. Imagine how well the voice matches the image of a beautiful, clear stream.
-   **Reference Audio (Neutral):** `797_127183_000017_000000.wav`
-   **Evaluation Criteria:**
    -   **1 (Clear):** A voice with minimal noise or breathiness, very clear and easy to understand. A transparent quality.
    -   **4 (Neutral):** An average level of hoarseness.
    -   **7 (Hoarse):** A voice with significant breathiness from the throat or a gravelly sound. Similar to a whisper. May also apply to a voice affected by a cold.

---

### 4. Composure (Calm - Restless)

-   **Scale:** Calm (1) - Restless (7)
-   **Overview:** This is an evaluation of the speaker's perceived mental state based on their speaking style and tone. Judge as if you were watching a presentation by an acquaintance.
-   **Reference Audio (Neutral):** `1100_124547_000014_000003.wav`
-   **Evaluation Criteria:**
    -   **1 (Calm):** A stable speaking pace with no vocal tremor, giving a calm and steady impression.
    -   **4 (Neutral):** A state that feels neither particularly calm nor restless.
    -   **7 (Restless):** A fast speaking pace, or a voice that is strained or trembling, conveying an impression of haste, tension, or anxiety.

---

### 5. Power (Powerful - Weak)

-   **Scale:** Powerful (1) - Weak (7)
-   **Overview:** This is an evaluation of the voice's power and presence. Consider not just the volume, but also the core strength of the voice. Imagine how much pressure you would feel when speaking face-to-face with this person.
-   **Reference Audio (Neutral):** `470_127690_000008_000006.wav`
-   **Evaluation Criteria:**
    -   **1 (Powerful):** A voice that is strong, full of confidence, and has a powerful energy that draws the listener in.
    -   **4 (Neutral):** A voice of average power.
    -   **7 (Weak):** A voice that is thin, quiet, and gives an unreliable impression. Sounds as if lacking confidence.

---

### 6. Perceived Age (Young - Aged)

-   **Scale:** Young (1) - Aged (7)
-   **Overview:** This is an evaluation of the speaker's perceived age from their voice. Imagine what age you would picture if you received a phone call from this person.
-   **Reference Audio (Neutral):** `22_121140_000011_000004.wav`
-   **Evaluation Criteria:**
    -   **1 (Young):** A voice with a very youthful impression, like that of a child or teenager.
    -   **4 (Neutral):** A voice that gives the impression of someone in their 30s, neither particularly young nor old.
    -   **7 (Aged):** A voice that gives the impression of an elderly person, characterized by raspiness or a very settled tone.

---

### 7. Thickness (Thick - Thin)

-   **Scale:** Thick (1) - Thin (7)
-   **Overview:** This is an evaluation of the richness of resonance and the qualitative thickness of the voice. Imagine the thickness of the "mass of voice" as it travels from the lungs, through the vocal tract, and out of the mouth.
-   **Reference Audio (Neutral):** `362_123013_000018_000002.wav`
-   **Evaluation Criteria:**
    -   **1 (Thick):** A solid, rich, deep voice with strong resonance. A voice that emerges thickly from the throat.
    -   **4 (Neutral):** A voice of average thickness.
    -   **7 (Thin):** A sharp, metallic voice with little resonance, giving an impression like a thin thread.

---

### 8. Vibrancy (Firm - Relaxed)

-   **Scale:** Firm (1) - Relaxed (7)
-   **Overview:** This is an evaluation of the voice's vitality and liveliness. Imagine the degree of tension in the person's abdominal muscles as they speak.
-   **Reference Audio (Neutral):** `283_130638_000051_000002.wav`
-   **Evaluation Criteria:**
    -   **1 (Firm):** A voice with a bouncing energy, giving a lively and vibrant impression.
    -   **4 (Neutral):** A voice with average vibrancy.
    -   **7 (Relaxed):** A voice lacking energy, monotonous, and giving a tired or listless impression.

---

### 9. Tone (Dark - Bright)

-   **Scale:** Dark (1) - Bright (7)
-   **Overview:** This is an evaluation of the psychological impression on the listener created by the voice's tone and atmosphere. Imagine you are a call center agent receiving a call and rate the impression.
-   **Reference Audio (Neutral):** `667_158816_000003_000000.wav`
-   **Evaluation Criteria:**
    -   **1 (Dark):** A voice that creates a heavy atmosphere, a serious mood, or a sense of tension.
    -   **4 (Neutral):** A voice with a neutral atmosphere, neither particularly dark nor bright.
    -   **7 (Bright):** A cheerful, lively, or positive-sounding voice that creates a bright atmosphere.

---

### 10. Warmth (Cold - Warm)

-   **Scale:** Cold (1) - Warm (7)
-   **Overview:** This is an evaluation of the speaker's perceived attitude and emotional distance from their voice. Imagine hearing this voice from a receptionist you are meeting for the first time at an office.
-   **Reference Audio (Neutral):** `122_121729_000028_000002.wav`
-   **Evaluation Criteria:**
    -   **1 (Cold):** A mechanical, emotionless voice that sounds dismissive or unfriendly.
    -   **4 (Neutral):** A voice that is neither cold nor warm, neither dismissive nor empathetic. A business-like tone.
    -   **7 (Warm):** A kind, affectionate voice that sounds empathetic and friendly.
